History

White water rafting as a commercial activity traces its roots to the early 20th century, but many iconic rafting sites have deeper historical significance. One such site is the Snake River in Wyoming, where the first recorded attempt to navigate its wild waters occurred in 1811—earning it the nickname “Mad River” due to its treacherous rapids. In 1940, Clyde Smith led the first successful commercial rafting trip through the Snake River Canyon, using inflatable pontoon rafts inspired by military designs. This pioneering journey helped establish the river as a premier destination for adventure tourism. By the 1950s, float trips were offered at resorts like Jackson Lake Lodge, and by the 1970s, rafting companies began forming across the U.S., transforming natural river corridors into thriving hubs for outdoor recreation. Today, sites like the Snake River continue to attract thrill-seekers, blending rugged history with modern eco-tourism.
